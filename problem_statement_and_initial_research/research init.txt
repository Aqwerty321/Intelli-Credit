Next-Generation Corporate Credit Decisioning: Comprehensive Architecture and Implementation Blueprint for the Intelli-Credit Challenge
The Paradigm Shift in Indian Corporate Lending
The Indian corporate lending ecosystem is currently defined by a severe operational bottleneck fundamentally characterized as the "Data Paradox".1 The rapid proliferation of digital public infrastructure, combined with aggressive regulatory digitization and widespread financial inclusion initiatives, has generated an unprecedented volume of verifiable financial data.1 Credit managers and underwriting teams now have access to highly granular structured datasets, such as Goods and Services Tax (GST) filings, Income Tax Returns (ITRs), and digital bank statements.1 Simultaneously, they are inundated with vast repositories of unstructured intelligence, encompassing annual reports, board meeting minutes, rating agency analyses, and statutory corporate filings.1 Despite this wealth of information, the manual aggregation, contextualization, and analysis of these disparate data streams routinely require weeks of dedicated effort to process for a single mid-sized corporate loan application.1
This profound operational friction arises because human analysts are inherently ill-equipped to perform web-scale data synthesis while simultaneously maintaining strict analytical rigor across thousands of discrete data points.1 Traditional underwriting processes evaluate historical financial statements in a vacuum, often failing to detect dynamic, early warning signals buried within unstructured text or distributed across external web sources.1 Furthermore, manual appraisal mechanisms are inherently susceptible to cognitive bias, fatigue, and human error, which directly translates to inconsistent risk premium assignments, suboptimal limit determinations, and elevated portfolio risk.1 When lenders rely on static, backward-looking financial snapshots, they fail to capture the dynamic reality of modern corporate financial health, where credit quality can pivot in days due to supply chain disruptions or sudden regulatory shifts.2
To bridge this critical intelligence gap, financial institutions require an artificial intelligence-powered Credit Decisioning Engine capable of automating the end-to-end preparation of a Comprehensive Credit Appraisal Memo (CAM).1 According to the specific operational constraints outlined for Round 1 of the Intelli-Credit Challenge on the Unstop platform, this solution must transition seamlessly from theoretical design to a working prototype.6 The documentation submission, mandated by the March 10, 2026 deadline, requires a presentation that meticulously details the technical architecture, core functionality, tools utilized, and potential real-world applications such as scalability and relevance.6 The ensuing architectural blueprint provides a definitive technical framework to construct this system, utilizing state-of-the-art data lakehouse paradigms, agentic artificial intelligence, and graph-theoretic fraud detection methodologies to satisfy and exceed all hackathon evaluation criteria.
Core Architectural Framework: The Data Intelligence Platform
The foundational infrastructure for the credit decisioning engine must support the simultaneous ingestion, transformation, and governance of structured, semi-structured, and unstructured data streams at massive scale. The architecture relies entirely on the Databricks Data Intelligence Platform, leveraging the Lakehouse paradigm to unify data engineering, analytics, and machine learning workloads into a singular, cohesive ecosystem.7
By deploying a Medallion Architecture—comprising Bronze, Silver, and Gold data layers—the system guarantees that raw financial data is systematically refined into highly curated, analytics-ready feature sets.10 The ingestion of multi-source data is orchestrated via Databricks Lakeflow and Spark Declarative Pipelines, which natively handle high-latency data streams while optimizing autonomously for computational performance and infrastructure cost.1 Raw API payloads from the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA), e-Courts, and credit bureaus are ingested into the Bronze layer as immutable event logs.12 The Silver layer performs deduplication, schema enforcement, and the parsing of complex unstructured PDFs into structured delta tables.10 Finally, the Gold layer aggregates these clean records into business-level entities, such as comprehensive corporate borrower profiles, standardized financial ratios, and pre-calculated risk vectors ready for machine learning inference.9
A critical component of this architectural foundation is the implementation of Unity Catalog, which enforces centralized metadata governance across all portfolio data, model outputs, and external intelligence overlays.10 In the context of financial services, where Personal Identifiable Information (PII) and corporate financial secrets are ubiquitous, Unity Catalog provides granular, row- and column-level access controls.10 Crucially, it automatically captures real-time data lineage, mapping exactly how an external GST filing or an extracted PDF table propagates through the transformation pipelines and into the final CAM recommendation.18 This traceable compound system ensures absolute audit readiness, allowing regulatory bodies to inspect the exact data vectors that influenced a specific credit decision, thereby transforming black-box algorithms into transparent, compliant underwriting mechanisms.20

Pillar I: The Data Ingestor and Multi-Format Synthesis
The first pillar of the decisioning engine demands a highly resilient mechanism to parse, extract, and synthesize the fragmented data formats native to the Indian financial ecosystem.1 This requires moving beyond simple data ingestion and implementing deep, context-aware data synthesis to extract actionable intelligence from both structured tax filings and deeply unstructured corporate documents.1
Unstructured Parsing and Intelligent Document Processing (IDP)
A significant portion of corporate credit due diligence relies on evaluating scanned, multi-page Indian-context PDFs, such as complex legal notices, sanction letters from competing banks, and comprehensive annual reports that frequently exceed a hundred pages.1 Traditional Optical Character Recognition (OCR) systems are fundamentally inadequate for this task, as they merely extract raw text without preserving the spatial semantics, hierarchical headings, or the intricate structural formatting of financial tables.23 When traditional OCR processes a balance sheet, it often flattens the columns, rendering the numerical data indistinguishable from the narrative footnotes, which completely breaks the downstream financial analysis.23
To address this critical failure point, the architecture employs AI-native Intelligent Document Processing (IDP) tools, such as Marker or Unstructured.io, tightly integrated with the Databricks processing engine.3 These cognitive extraction engines do not merely read characters; they interpret the intent, structure, and relational geometry of the document.23 By utilizing advanced computer vision models and natural language processing (NLP), the ingestor reconstructs complex, broken, or merged financial tables from poor-quality scans, ensuring that critical data points—such as contingent liabilities, auditor qualifications, or specific financial covenants—are accurately mapped into structured JSON or Delta tables.3 This extraction accuracy is a primary evaluation metric for the hackathon, as the downstream recommendation engine's reliability is entirely dependent on the high-fidelity parsing of these unstructured artifacts.1
Structured Data Synthesis: The GSTR-2A and 3B Reconciliation Mechanism
In the Indian corporate context, evaluating the authenticity of a borrower's self-declared revenue requires deep, automated cross-verification between statutory tax filings and actual cash flows.1 The system executes a rigorous programmatic reconciliation between GSTR-2A and GSTR-3B filings.3 GSTR-2A serves as an auto-populated, read-only statement reflecting inward supplies based directly on the filings of registered counterparties and suppliers, effectively detailing the maximum Input Tax Credit (ITC) available at a highly granular invoice level.24 Conversely, GSTR-3B is a monthly, self-declared summary return reflecting actual purchases, gross tax liabilities, and the ITC that the entity has actually claimed.24
The ingestion pipeline automatically cross-leverages these filings. A significant divergence between the two forms serves as an immediate, highly predictive early warning signal.24 For instance, if the ITC claimed in the self-declared GSTR-3B significantly exceeds the supplier-verified ITC visible in GSTR-2A, it strongly flags potential over-claiming of credits, engagement with non-compliant "ghost" suppliers, or severe cash flow distress driving aggressive tax avoidance.24 The architecture programmatically identifies specific reasons for non-reconciliation, successfully isolating legitimate timing differences or reverse charge mechanism claims from actual fraudulent reporting.24
This sophisticated tax logic is subsequently cross-referenced against the parsed, digitized bank statements.1 By computationally pairing GST-reported operational turnover with actual bank inflows, the system exposes hidden liquidity gaps and shadow transactions, ensuring that reported sales are backed by verifiable cash movements.1 This dual verification drastically reduces the institution's reliance on easily manipulated, self-declared financial numbers.27
Advanced Fraud Topology: Graph-Theoretic Circular Trading Detection
A highly sophisticated method of artificially inflating corporate revenue and manipulating credit limits in India is "circular trading." In this scenario, a network of colluding dealers superimposes suspicious transactions with illegitimate sales in a continuous, circular loop—for example, Company A sells goods to Company B, Company B sells to Company C, and Company C sells back to Company A.28 This is done entirely on paper without any actual value addition or physical movement of underlying goods, solely to inflate the top-line revenue of all participating entities and artificially generate input tax credits.28 Standard relational databases and basic rule engines severely struggle to detect these patterns because every individual transaction appears legitimate in isolation.29
The architectural solution natively implements advanced Graph Analytics utilizing Databricks GraphFrames to completely dismantle this fraud topology.30 By modeling the financial ecosystem as a vast, directed multigraph, the system treats corporate entities, GSTINs, and specific Bank Accounts as vertices, while treating invoices and fund transfers as directed, weighted edges.32
To definitively detect circular trading, the system deploys highly efficient motif finding algorithms natively supported by GraphFrames.31 A motif query, expressed syntactically in the architecture as (a)-[e1]->(b); (b)-[e2]->(c); (c)-[e3]->(a), programmatically isolates cyclical paths of capital flow across the entire transactional network.31 When the algorithm detects high-velocity, short-cycle networks characterized by synchronized turnovers and overlapping corporate directorships, it immediately flags the entire cluster for circular trading.33 Advanced Label Propagation Algorithms (LPA) are subsequently utilized to identify broader communities of colluding actors connected to the primary fraud ring.31 This graph-based feature extraction feeds directly into the machine learning scoring model, dynamically increasing the borrower's risk premium to prohibitive levels or triggering an outright, fully documented rejection.1
Pillar II: The Digital Credit Manager and Agentic Workflows
The second pillar of the Intelli-Credit Challenge requires the deployment of a "Digital Credit Manager"—an autonomous, AI-driven research agent designed to perform web-scale secondary research and seamlessly integrate qualitative primary insights from human operators.1 This agent moves far beyond static data retrieval or basic search indexing; it utilizes the Databricks Mosaic AI Agent Framework to operate with profound contextual awareness, strategic reasoning, and tool-calling capabilities.8
Autonomous Secondary Intelligence Gathering
The research agent functions via a sophisticated Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture integrated deeply with advanced function-calling capabilities.36 To comprehensively evaluate a corporate entity's risk profile, the agent autonomously orchestrates parallel queries across a vast array of external Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) and targeted web crawlers:
Firstly, utilizing web-scale crawling tools such as Tavily or Exa, the agent scours unstructured news sources, financial portals, and industry publications for macroeconomic headwinds, regulatory shifts, and nuanced sentiment analysis regarding the company's promoters.1 For example, the agent can actively search for and interpret the impact of "new RBI regulations on NBFCs" on the specific borrower's business model, adjusting risk parameters accordingly.1
Secondly, the agent conducts rigorous corporate and legal due diligence by directly interfacing with India-specific governmental and judicial endpoints.1 It queries the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) verification APIs to extract real-time, authoritative data on corporate registration status, historical balance sheets, cross-directorships, and active financial charges or mortgages registered against the company's assets.13 Simultaneously, it connects to the e-Courts India API to trace the comprehensive litigation history of both the borrowing entity and its key promoters.1 By executing Case Number Record (CNR) searches, the agent pulls details on ongoing legal disputes, bounced cheques, or contractual breaches that could imminently threaten the company's operational viability.41
Thirdly, the agent queries the CIBIL Commercial Report API to retrieve standardized credit risk assessments, historical default records, and the broader institutional credit footprint of the non-individual borrower.43 This provides an instant baseline of the entity's historical adherence to debt obligations.43
The Mosaic AI Agent Framework continuously evaluates the retrieved data for accuracy, relevance, and toxicity, completely preventing model hallucinations by anchoring the generative process strictly to the verified, retrieved context stored within the Unity Catalog.8
Primary Insight Integration and Dynamic Risk Adjustment
Quantitative metrics and API payloads alone are insufficient for holistic, highly accurate credit appraisal. Real-world commercial underwriting relies heavily on primary due diligence—nuanced observations gathered during physical factory site visits, inventory inspections, or management interviews.1
The architecture explicitly provisions a specialized, intuitive user interface portal for the Credit Officer to input qualitative notes in natural language, such as stating that a "Factory was found operating at forty percent capacity due to severe machinery breakdown and labor unrest".1 The digital credit manager leverages advanced natural language understanding to parse these unstructured qualitative inputs, mapping the semantic sentiment to specific, quantifiable risk vectors within the model architecture, such as capacity utilization degradation or acute operational risk.1
The underlying AI must then dynamically adjust the final quantitative risk score based on these human-provided nuances.1 If the primary insights indicate severe, undocumented operational distress, the agent communicates this binding constraint directly to the recommendation engine, overriding otherwise positive signals derived purely from historical structured data. This seamless fusion of deterministic quantitative data with probabilistic qualitative human assessment flawlessly mimics the highly nuanced reasoning of a senior credit committee, ensuring that the AI acts as a collaborative co-pilot rather than an isolated decision maker.2
Pillar III: The Explainable Recommendation Engine and CAM Generator
The culmination of the Intelli-Credit architecture is the Recommendation Engine, responsible for synthesizing the massive volume of processed data into a transparent, mathematically sound lending decision and an automated Credit Appraisal Memo (CAM).1
Generating the Credit Appraisal Memo (CAM)
The manual drafting of a CAM is highly repetitive, excessively time-consuming, and prone to structural inconsistencies across different underwriting teams.46 The AI-powered CAM Generator utilizes a highly optimized Large Language Model (LLM) pipeline to automatically author a professional, rigorously structured document in standard Word or PDF formats.1 This document exhaustively summarizes the foundational Five Cs of Credit: Character, Capacity, Capital, Collateral, and Conditions.1
Through strict prompt engineering paradigms and the provision of static analytical templates, the LLM is tightly constrained to output highly formal, banking-standard narratives.46 Structured inputs, such as the calculated financial liquidity ratios, debt-service coverage ratios, and the GST/Bank reconciliation summaries, flow directly into the quantitative subsections of the memo.46 Simultaneously, the unstructured insights gathered by the Research Agent—such as negative news trends or active litigation history—are elegantly synthesized into contextual narratives outlining the borrower's "Character" and market "Conditions".46 The resulting memo is a fully realized, interactive draft that perfectly standardizes the financial institution's analytical format while drastically reducing the turnaround time from several weeks to mere minutes.46
Calibrated Scoring Models and Risk Premium Determination
The core decision logic—specifically suggesting whether to lend, the exact loan limit, and the precise risk-adjusted interest rate—cannot rely on simple heuristic rules or uncalibrated generative text output.1 The architecture deploys advanced machine learning models, specifically calibrated Gradient Boosting Machines such as XGBoost or LightGBM, trained extensively on historical default and delinquency data.22
These models ingest the massive feature matrix constructed in Pillar 1, which includes GST mismatches, circular trading motif flags, and cash flow volatility metrics, alongside the risk vectors quantified in Pillar 2, such as litigation severity scores and CIBIL Commercial risk ratings.22 The algorithm utilizes a sophisticated cost-aware threshold selection mechanism to output a probabilistic assessment of default.51 Based on the predicted risk tier, the engine applies rigorous pricing logic to suggest an appropriate risk premium and dynamically sizes the credit limit to optimize the institution's capital allocation and minimize potential loss given default.1
The Imperative of Explainable AI (XAI)
A fundamental evaluation criterion for the hackathon—and a strict, non-negotiable regulatory requirement in modern financial services globally—is complete model explainability.1 "Black-box" AI models that output loan rejections without accessible, logical reasoning severely undermine customer trust, violate fair lending practices, and expose financial institutions to catastrophic compliance risks and regulatory sanctions.22
To fully satisfy the explicit requirement that the AI must "walk the judge through its logic," the recommendation engine integrates a dedicated Explainability Agent utilizing SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP) and Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations (LIME) frameworks.1
SHAP operates on the principles of cooperative game theory, calculating the exact marginal contribution of every single feature to the final algorithmic prediction.51 The mathematical foundation, represented by the Shapley value formulation , ensures that the attribution of importance is perfectly fair and mathematically sound. SHAP provides both global and local interpretability, identifying exactly how much a specific data point, such as an e-Courts litigation record, pushed the model toward a rejection decision.53 Complementing this, LIME provides local proximity diagnoses by subtly perturbing the input data and observing the corresponding changes in the prediction, creating a simplified, highly human-readable linear model around a single, specific loan application.51
When the machine learning model recommends a credit rejection or a severely reduced limit, the Explainability Agent instantly parses the underlying SHAP and LIME values to identify the heavily weighted negative features.22 It then passes these numerical vectors to the generative LLM, which translates the complex mathematical attributions into plain-text financial reasoning.22 For example, the engine natively generates explanations such as: "Rejected due to high litigation risk found in secondary research despite strong GST flows," thereby transforming an opaque algorithmic output into a fully auditable "glass-box" decision.1 This entire lifecycle is tracked using Databricks MLflow, which logs all model parameters, SHAP artifacts, training runs, and evaluation datasets, ensuring that the explainability metrics are tightly version-controlled and permanently preserved for future regulatory audits.20
Hackathon Alignment: SPAIN and HEART Frameworks
To ensure maximum performance against the hackathon evaluation criteria, the architecture has been rigorously validated against the SPAIN and HEART frameworks.55
The SPAIN framework dictates the core engineering principles of the solution.55 Stability is achieved through the Databricks Lakehouse architecture, which guarantees reliable operation even when processing millions of unstructured documents.9 Performance is optimized via Spark Declarative Pipelines and Delta Lake's rapid querying capabilities.11 Availability is ensured by deploying the model serving endpoints as scalable REST APIs, capable of handling real-time credit checks seamlessly.9 Integrity is the cornerstone of the system, maintained through Unity Catalog's strict access controls, PII masking, and real-time data lineage.16 Finally, Novelty is demonstrated through the innovative application of GraphFrames for detecting circular trading and the use of SHAP-calibrated agents to translate mathematical models into human-readable credit memos.55
For the prototype documentation submission and subsequent demonstration, the strategy strictly adheres to the HEART framework.56 The presentation will be Hands-On, allowing judges to directly interact with the Digital Credit Manager portal to input primary insights.56 The Explanation will focus heavily on the "glass-box" nature of the AI, proving that the software speaks for itself through transparent SHAP values.56 The system's Adaptability will be showcased by perturbing the input data live to demonstrate how the risk premium dynamically updates.56 The entire prototype relies exclusively on Real Data, utilizing anonymized but authentic GST filings, bank statements, and active e-Courts API payloads to ensure maximum relevance.56 Finally, rigorous Testing protocols via MLflow evaluation datasets guarantee that the demo performs flawlessly under scrutiny.56

Strategic Synthesis and Implementation Impact
The realization of the Intelli-Credit Challenge through this comprehensive architectural blueprint represents a fundamental paradigm shift in corporate lending operations. By decisively transitioning from highly manual, strictly siloed workflows to an interconnected, agentic intelligence platform, financial institutions can definitively eliminate the systemic bottlenecks that define the contemporary data paradox.
The rigorous implementation of the Databricks platform as the core data intelligence layer ensures that the sheer volume of high-latency structured data—such as transactional bank statements and API-driven tax filings—is ingested, governed, and mapped without any degradation in processing speed or data integrity. The integration of GraphFrames fundamentally alters the landscape of financial fraud detection, upgrading the institution's capability from merely identifying static transactional anomalies to proactively mapping dynamic, multi-hop circular trading networks that actively obscure true corporate liquidity.
Furthermore, the deployment of the Mosaic AI Agent Framework effectively digitizes the investigative capacity of a senior human credit analyst, executing comprehensive due diligence across the open web, statutory databases, and judicial portals in mere seconds. Finally, by strictly adhering to an Explainable AI (XAI) framework via SHAP and MLflow, the system achieves total regulatory compliance and fosters absolute user trust, delivering fully automated Credit Appraisal Memos that do not merely generate algorithmic edicts, but rather provide fully reasoned, context-aware, and highly defensible financial judgments. This architecture successfully addresses every demand of the hackathon, yielding an enterprise-ready, India-context-sensitive credit decisioning engine capable of redefining the future of corporate underwriting.
